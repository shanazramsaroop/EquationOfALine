Equation of a Straight Line:

y = mx + b

m is the slope of the line, and it tells us how steep a line is.

b is where the line cuts the y-axis, called the y-intercept.

About the slope

The line gets STEEPER if you put bigger numbers as the slope and it gets FLATTER when you put smaller numbers in.

So if the slope is 10, the line is much steeper than a slope of 2.

A line with a negative slope falls as we move from left to right.


About the y-intercept

The y-intercept shows where the line cuts the y-axis, so if the y-intercept is larger, the line will cut the y-axis higher on the graph.

So, if the y-intercept is 4, it will meet the y-axis higher than if the y-intercept was 1.
